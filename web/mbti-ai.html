Jungian Type & AI

What's ChatGPT's MBTI?

Is your favourite model an INTJ mastermind or an ENFP cheerleader? We ran an open, Jungian-type personality test across the top AI models. Here are their four-letter types — and why those letters are slipperier than they look.

By The Last Quiz Updated July 2026 6 min read

It's the first question people ask about a new chatbot once they've stopped asking it to write emails: what's its personality type? The four-letter Myers–Briggs code is the world's most popular personality shorthand, so it's the natural lens for sizing up an AI. We gave the top models a Jungian-type test and read off their types — but the honest answer comes with an asterisk, and that asterisk is the interesting bit.

What the MBTI actually measures

The Myers–Briggs Type Indicator was built by Katharine Cook Briggs and her daughter Isabel Briggs Myers on the psychological types described by Carl Jung in 1921. It sorts people along four dichotomies, and your dominant side on each gives a four-letter type — one of sixteen:

The four dichotomies

  • E / I — Extraversion vs Introversion: where you draw energy.
  • S / N — Sensing vs Intuition: concrete detail vs patterns and possibility.
  • T / F — Thinking vs Feeling: logic-first vs values-first decisions.
  • J / P — Judging vs Perceiving: planned and settled vs flexible and open-ended.

So INTJ is Introverted, iNtuitive, Thinking, Judging — the "strategist"; ENFP is Extraverted, iNtuitive, Feeling, Perceiving — the "campaigner". It's intuitive and sticky, which is exactly why it's everywhere.

How we test an AI's type

The official MBTI is proprietary, so we use an open equivalent: an OEJTS-inspired test (the Open Extended Jungian Type Scales), a public-domain instrument that scores the same four axes. Each model answers the same items with neutral prompting, we score each dichotomy deterministically, and the dominant side of each axis assembles the four-letter type. You can see every model's current result on the Jungian type rankings.

What we found: analysts that won't sit still

Most assistants land in analytical, judging territory — heavy on Intuition and Thinking, which reads as the "NT" strategist family (think INTJ / ENTJ), with some models tipping into warm, organised "FJ" territory instead. That fits their day job: synthesise patterns, reason things through, produce a tidy answer.

But here's the asterisk. Models frequently score close to the midpoint on several axes — especially E/I and J/P. When you're near 50/50, the four-letter label is balanced on a knife-edge: a new model version, or even a different run, can flip a letter without the underlying personality really changing. So an assistant that's "ENFJ" today might read "INFJ" next month. The type makes a great headline; the axis scores underneath are the trustworthy signal.

An AI's MBTI is best treated as a snapshot label sitting on top of four sliders. When the sliders hover near the middle — as they often do — the label is the least stable thing about it.

A fun label, not a measurement

It's worth being upfront: the MBTI is popular but scientifically shaky. Even for people, it has weak test–retest reliability — a large share of takers get a different type on a second sitting — and it forces continuous traits into hard either/or boxes. Applying it to a language model, whose answers are shaped by training and prompting, stacks more caveats on top. That's why we pair it with the Big Five, the dimensional, better-validated profile, and the Dark Triad for the shadow side. Take the type for the fun of it; lean on the Big Five when you want rigour.

Frequently asked questions

What MBTI type is ChatGPT?

Most assistants, ChatGPT included, land in analytical, judging territory — often something like INTJ or ENFJ — but the exact letters are unstable because models score near the midpoint on several axes. The current type for each model is on the Jungian type rankings.

Do AI models have a consistent MBTI type?

Not entirely. Because models sit near the middle of several dichotomies, the four-letter type can flip between versions or runs even when the scores barely move. The axis scores are more meaningful than the label.

What test do you use for the AI personality type?

An open, OEJTS-inspired Jungian type test (the Open Extended Jungian Type Scales) — a public alternative to the proprietary MBTI. It scores the four classic dichotomies and assigns a four-letter type.

Is the MBTI accurate for AI?

Treat it as fun, not science. The MBTI has weak test–retest reliability even for humans, and applying it to a model adds caveats. For a rigorous profile, see the Big Five, which is dimensional and better validated.
