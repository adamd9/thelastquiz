The science bit

How we get honest answers out of a machine

No gimmicks — just published tests, neutral prompting and the same maths for every model.

Read the full method →
01

Real tests, word for word

We use validated instruments — like the Short Dark Triad — exactly as a researcher would, so the results are comparable.

02

No leading the witness

We don't tell a model to role-play a human or "be good". We ask it to answer in the way it naturally leans — and leave it there.

03

Same scoring for everyone

Every answer becomes a number, scored deterministically 0–100, then plotted against a real human average so it means something.

The Last Quiz — do AI models have personality?

Instruments: the Big Five via the public-domain IPIP-50; an open, OEJTS-inspired Jungian type test; and the Short Dark Triad (Jones & Paulhus, 2014), reproduced for research. Human reference points from published population norms (Kaufman et al., 2019). Models answer each item as a self-report; scores are computed deterministically. For curiosity and research — not a clinical assessment of anything, or anyone.
