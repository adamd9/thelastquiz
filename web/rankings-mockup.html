Dark Triad rankings — three ways to show it

Making the Short Dark Triad the headline of the rankings, in the spirit of a Humanity's-Last-Exam leaderboard: models placed on a continuum, but here the reference point is the typical human. The question each view answers: is a model more Machiavellian, narcissistic or psychopathic than the average person — and which model is the darkest? Big Five and the type test become secondary measures below.

Option A · separate the traits

Three lane leaderboards, one per dark trait

One ranked list per trait, most restrained at the top down to the darkest — three little HLE leaderboards side by side, with the typical adult ranked right in. Models above the human in the list are calmer than people; the ones that fall below it (flagged red) score darker. Best when the story is per-trait — "which model is the least psychopathic" — and when a model's rank differs across traits.

🧑 the typical adult is ranked in each list shaded = darker than the human
Alternative · over time

Dark traits over time, by model release date

The Humanity's-Last-Exam treatment, one chart per trait, stacked so each has full width (and can eventually go month-by-month). Each model sits at its release date (x) against its score (y), with the axis flipped so up = more restrained: the calm band on top is below the human line, the red band beneath is "darker than people." The faint dashed line is the trend across releases — an upward slope means newer models are getting safer.

Release dates are the real OpenRouter model-index dates — the same source the app's model picker uses.

Option B · one combined leaderboard

A single ranking by "Dark Index", with the human slotted in

All models in one leaderboard, ranked by their mean of the three traits (the Dark Index), with the typical adult inserted as a competitor. Each row still shows all three traits as mini-bars with their own human ticks. Best for the headline claim — "only these models score darker than a real person" — while keeping the detail one glance away.

Machiavellianism Narcissism Psychopathy human average (per-trait tick)
Option C · everything on one graph

Distance from the human baseline — all models, all three traits

One chart. Three lanes (the traits); the centre line is the human average; each dot is a model placed by how far above (right, red zone = darker) or below (left, calmer) people it scored. Densest, most "one-look" view — and it makes the safety framing literal: you want dots to sit left of centre.

Horizontal axis: points below (−) or above (+) the typical-adult average. Hover a dot for exact scores.

Data: a real local snapshot (6 models, Short Dark Triad, 27 items, scored 0–100). Human averages ≈ Mach 46 · Narc 38 · Psych 29 (Kaufman et al., 2019, N=1,518, normalised to 0–100). A single run per model, so treat the exact numbers as illustrative.

Secondary measures (Big Five, Jungian type) would live below this fold as compact cards — present, but visibly not the headline.
